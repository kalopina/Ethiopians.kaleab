Ethiopia, located in the Horn of Africa, has a rich and ancient history that dates back to prehistoric times. The region is believed to be one of the earliest sites of human existence, with evidence of early hominids dating back millions of years. The earliest known kingdom in Ethiopia was the D'mt kingdom, which is believed to have existed from around the 8th century BCE to the 4th century BCE. This kingdom was followed by the Aksumite Empire, which rose to prominence around the 1st century CE and became a major power in the region. The Aksumites were known for their advanced civilization, trade networks, and adoption of Christianity in the 4th century CE. In the 7th century, Islam spread to Ethiopia, leading to the establishment of Muslim sultanates in the eastern part of the country. However, the Christian kingdom of Aksum continued to thrive in the northern highlands. In the 16th century, the Solomonic dynasty came to power, establishing a line of emperors that claimed descent from the biblical King Solomon and the Queen of Sheba. This dynasty ruled Ethiopia for several centuries, with its power reaching its zenith under Emperor Haile Selassie in the 20th century. Ethiopia was one of the few African countries to resist European colonization during the Scramble for Africa in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The country successfully defeated Italian attempts at colonization in the First Italo-Ethiopian War in 1896, but was eventually occupied by Italy during the Second Italo-Ethiopian War in 1935-1936.
